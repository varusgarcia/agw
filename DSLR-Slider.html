DSLR-Slider Tutorial

Here I'm going to walk you through what you need to know to make your own slider for DSLR cameras. If you don't need such a big slider, please check out my tutorial for my mini-slider.
This slider can be used with or without a motor for filming video. If you want to shoot timelapses with it, you will need to motorize it.
The main material used for building the frame and rail is aluminium, because of strenght and lightweight.
I have some experience on building DIY sliders, and i can say that you are never going to achieve that professional smoothness that you get with bought sliders, but that, spending a lot less you get a pretty good result

Tutorial Overview

Here I give you an overview of what you will achive if you follow the tutorial. It also shows again the materials needed

Step 01: Getting the materials.

You might not find all the materials I used in your Hardware Store, like the Bearings and the tripod bolt and nut. I myself bought them on Ebay, so you might want to do the same. Just search with the title I used and maybe the diameter, making sure the specs are the same. If you can't find it, just contact me.
You also might find a similar material, but not totally the same as mine, then you have to adjust all the measurments to your materials.

Step 02: Cutting & Drilling

Alright, now it's time to cut up the aluminium plates, if you haven't done that already. You can choose another lenght for the dolly, in my case 163mm, if you take that measurment into account for the drilling.
For the drilling, I would recommend to follow my instructions and measurments, because there are more things that you have to consider so that all the bearings touch the rail. The holes for the bolts are either 4mm or 8mm (only twice in the side of the L plate), then there is the tripod mount hole (in the middle of one od the top plates), which is also 8mm

Top Plates

These three plates will hold the two L plates together. In between them the threaded rods that hold the bearings are going to be fixated. These bearings are going to prevent the dolly from moving around.

"L" Plates

You will need to make two of these. They are going to hold the bearings so that the dolly can slide up and down. Because of that, you need to be as precise as possible with the drilling. After the drilling is done, you have to cut out rectangular pieces from the top face. (as shown in the drillsheet).

Drilling Sheet

These are the exact measures for the drilling. All the measures are in mm The holes have either 4mm or 8mm in diameter.

Step 03: Assembling

Now that you have everithing ready, lets assemble the dolly. There are some things that need to be done first:

Preparing the bearings:

To make sure that the dolly will slide properly, you have to make sure that you get this step right, beacuse otherwie, the bearings won't be aligned or will get loose. Getting this right is a matter patience and, if you don't make sure that the distances are right, you may spend a lot more time asembling the dolly, because you might have to undo some of your work.
PRO TIP: For the thread rods, you might find it difficult to srew in the Nylo Nuts, because you only have one point to apply force. Just set two nyloc nuts at the other end of the rods, and fix your tool to the second one. Now, you have two points to apply force to.

Assembling the Dolly

Now that everything is prepared, let's proceed to assemble the dolly. To further expalin the graphic below, there are some photos of the dolly fully assembled.

Assembling the Slider

The slider is much more easy to assemble. It consists of two rectangular tubes and two aluminium plates that hold them at 82mm of distance from eachother. They are hold together by two 4mm screws/bolts in each corner. Below you can find a drillingsheet for the tubes and the plates and a graphic of it.

Now that the dolly is asssembled, you can try out if everything works by placing it on the rail. In my case, at first the side bearings weren't tight enought, so i just pressed the four bolts together with my hands, and that fixed it.

Tripod and head mount:

If you want to mount the slider on a tripod, I recommend you to buy at least two Tripod Nuts (just search "1/4 BSW x5/8" Whitworth Nuts" on Ebay), drill a hole in the middle of each of the two plates that hold the rail together and glue the nut on top with two-component glue.
On the dolly, you might want some sort of tripod head, so just buy a 5/8" Whitwort screw
