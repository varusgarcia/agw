Mini-Slider Tutorial

This is a tutorial on how to do your own mini-slider for GoPro or any other lightweight camera out there. It's not ment to be a step by step tutorial, because you are probably not going to have the exact same materials I used. Because of that, it's more an extended overview on how I did it, so you can get a good picture on what you really need to acomplish a good result. I have some experience on building sliders, mainly bigger motorized sliders for video and Timelapse. From that experience I've learned that with a DIY slider you are never going to achieve that professional smoothness that you get with bought sliders, but that, spending a lot less you get a pretty good result. For now, this tutorial will cover how to make an hand-powered slider. Maybe later on I will extend it to show how you can motorize it.

Tutorial Overview

Here I give you an overview of what you will achive if you follow the tutorial. It also shows again the materials needed

Step 01: Getting the materials.

You might not find all the materials I used in your Hardware Store, like the Bearings and the tripod bolt and nut. I myself bought them on Ebay, so you might want to do the same. Just search with the title I used and maybe the diameter, making sure the specs are the same. If you can't find it, just contact me.
You also might find a similar material, but not totally the same as mine, then you have to adjust all the measurments to your materials.

Step 02: Cutting & Drilling

Alright, now it's time to cut up the aluminium tube and plates, if you haven't done that already. You can choose another lenght for the dolly, in my case 86mm., if you take that measurment into account for the drilling.
For the drilling, I would recommend to follow my instructions and measurments, because there are more things that you have to consider so that all the bearings touch the rail. The holes for the bolts are all 4mm., the only one that is different is the one for the tripod mount, which is 6mm.

Top Plate

This plate will hold the two L plates together. It's also were the tripod mount is going to be fixated.

"L" Plates

You will need to make two of these. They are going to hold the bearings so that the dolly can slide up and down. Because of that, you need to be as precise as possible with the drilling. After the drilling is done, you have to cut out rectangular pieces from the side face. This is so that the bearings don't touch the L Plate. It's better if you just cut out just as much as you need so that that doesn't happen.

Drilling sheet

These are the exact measures for the drilling. All the measures are in mm. The holes have all 4mm in diameter except the the one at thee center of the Top plate, which has 6mm.

Step 03: assembling the dolly

Now that you have everithing ready, lets assemble the dolly. There are some things that need to be done first:

Preparing the bearings:

To make sure that the dolly will slide properly, you have to make sure that you get this step right, beacuse otherwie, the bearings won't be aligned or will get loose. Getting this right is a matter patience and, if you don't make sure that the distances are right, you may spend a lot more time asembling the dolly, because you might undo some of your work.
PRO TIP: For the thread rod, you might find it difficult to srew in the Nylo Nuts, because you only have one point to apply force. Just set two nyloc nuts at the other end of the thread rod, and fix your tool to the second one. Now, you have two points to apply force to.

Assembling the Dolly

Now that everything is prepared, let's proceed to assemble the dolly. To further expalin the graphic below, there are some photos of the dolly fully assembled as well as a 3 view graphic below of that.

Now that the dolly is asssembled, you can try out if everything works by placing it on the rectangular tube. In my case, at first the side bearings weren't tight enought, so i just pressed the four bolts together with my hands, and that fixed it.

Tripod and head mount:

If you want to mount the slider on a tripod, I recommend you to buy at least two Tripod Nuts (just search "1/4 BSW x5/8" Whitworth Nuts" on Ebay), drill a hole trough the bottom of the aluminum rectangular pipe and glue the nut from inside with two-component glue.
On the dolly, you might want some sort of tripod head. What I use is the tripod adapter from GoPro fixed to the dolly with a tripod screw and a washer (just search "1/4 BSW x5/8" Whitworth Bolt" on Ebay). To rotate the GoPro, y just apply force.
