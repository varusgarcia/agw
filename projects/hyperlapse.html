Hyperlapse Tutorial

Hyperapse is the technique of taking timelapses while in motion. We could say that the slider technique is a Hyperlapse with minor movement between each picture. Because we don't normally have a 100m long slider (for that I have another Tutorial, in which I explain how you can do your own Timelapse-Cablecam), we have to position the camera with a tripod and move it by hand. There are some new and expensive gadgets that will do that for us like the Camtrac. Also, the trend is to forget about the technique and leave all the work for the stabilisation software, like the Google Street Hyperlapse Project, "Hyperlapse" from Instagram and "Microsoft Hyperlapse". Smartphones are getting better at this because of the advanced sensors but there is a level that just cant be achived with such softwares and the results will almost always seem artificial and have distorsion. To achive that professional look, some time has to be invested in the process and it's going to need some practice. I hope i can teach you all what I've learned from my failed attempts.

What you need:

DSLR or compact camera

DSLR is a better for this because the lens has some more options, such as zoom, focus control...

Tripod

this gadget is not really necessary, but it adds stabilization for more professional results

Computer and editing software

You will need at least an mid-level computer with Adobe After effects installed. Premiere Pro will only work for the "imaginary line Method"

Preparations

Before you start with the shooting, we recommend you to decide what method want to use, either the "Fixed Point Method" or the "Imaginary Line Method". There is a third method that is more experimental and not so common, we'll call it "Stop Motion Method".So, what is the difference? It’s basically that in the first method, the camera maintains a point of reference from the beginning to the end at the same position. In the second one the camera maintains its position by imagining a straight line and mainlining it with the tripod. We'll explain which method is more convenient in which situation and show some examples of each.

Fixed point method

The Fixed point method is easier when you are shooting but requires more editing. It is the best for moving in circles or other shapes, as long as you don't lose the point of reference. You can also change the point of reference but we recommend to always follow the same one, because this is the only way to stabilize the whole video later. It gives a more static result but it allows you to center the attention of the viewer in your subject. This is a better solution if you want to make a video about a specific monument, for example, and you want to center the viewers attention to it. The editing part of the process requires a certain skill and some time, depending also on the quality of the photographs.In the video you can see some examples of how it looks.

For the last one, you'll need an additional point of reference.You can also try to stabilize the Hyperlapse with the warp stabilizer in Adobe After Effects or Premiere Pro. This method is fully automatic and requires no skills

Imaginary Line Method

The Imaginary line method changes in the way you move, you'll need to pay more attention on where you place the tripod and in which direction you shoot. It’s more complex to shoot but requires less skill on the editing side. Because of that, we recommend to search for some lines that can help you, like some kind of tile arrangement on the ground that you can use as reference. It is really easy to stabilize a hyperlapse shot this way, because you can always use the warp stabilizer in Adobe After Effects or in Adobe Premiere Pro and it does the hard work for you. Stop Motion

This method consists on moving the camera and the point of reference at the same time. This point of reference can be a person that moves with a music track or an object that you move for each shot. It may seem confusing at first but gives a really cool result.
The greatest example is the video Dream Music Part II by PermagrimFilms. They have also done a really cool Behind the Scenes video in which they explain how the achived such good results.
