Mini-Slider Tutorial

This is a tutorial on how to do your own mini-slider for GoPro or any other lightweight camera out there. It's not ment to be a step by step tutorial, because you are probably not going to have the exact same materials I used. Because of that, it's more an extended overview on how I did it, so you can get a good picture on what you really need to acomplish a good result. I have some experience on building sliders, mainly bigger motorized sliders for video and Timelapse. From that experience I've learned that with a DIY slider you are never going to achieve that professional smoothness that you get with bought sliders, but that, spending a lot less you get a pretty good result. For now, this tutorial will cover how to make an hand-powered slider. Maybe later on I will extend it to show how you can motorize it.

Tripod and head mount:

If you want to mount the slider on a tripod, I recommend you to buy at least two Tripod Nuts (just search "1/4 BSW x5/8" Whitworth Nuts" on Ebay), drill a hole trough the bottom of the aluminum rectangular pipe and glue the nut with two-component glue.
On the dolly, you might want some sort of tripod head. What I did was use the tripod adapter from GoPro fixed to the dolly with a tripod screw and a washer (just search "1/4 BSW x5/8" Whitworth Bolt" on Ebay). To rotate the GoPro, y just apply force.
